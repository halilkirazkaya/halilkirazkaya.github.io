I didn't actually have any near-term plans to pursue the CRTA (Certified Red Team Analyst) certification in my cybersecurity career. However, when I saw the Valentine's Day campaign from CyberWarfare Labs (CRTA and CRTSv2 combined for just $49!), I decided to seize the opportunity and temporarily shifted my focus here.

In this post, I will transparently share my entirely unplanned CRTA preparation process, my thoughts on the lab environment, and why the exam felt too "CTF-like" to me.

## Preparation and Lab Environment

When you purchase the certification, you get 30 days of lab access. Overall, I can say that I really enjoyed the lab environment. It offers an educational space to practice Active Directory and offensive concepts. Consuming the training materials and the lab will definitely add value to your skillset.

The only part I found a bit odd was the "Initial Access" section; it felt artificial rather than a real-world scenario. But here's the most ironic part: In my opinion, the lab environment was far more suitable to be an "exam" than the actual exam I took.

## Exam Structure and Planning

There is no reporting requirement in the CRTA. The exam structure isn't exactly "flag-based" (capture the flag) either; it's more like they ask you for something specific (such as a particular piece of data or value), and you enter that specific information into the system.

Your total exam duration is 6 hours. If you want to schedule the exam, there's an important operational detail you should know: You submit your exam time slot through the system, and the earliest you can start is 3 hours later. It's worth planning your schedule accordingly.

## My Exam Experience

On exam day, I logged in around 9:00 PM. My expectation was to have a step-by-step penetration testing experience similar to the training labs' structure.

However, I can't say I enjoyed the exam much. The reason was that it felt excessively "CTF-like" (Capture The Flag style). Rather than feeling like I was performing lateral movement in a real Active Directory network, there was an atmosphere of solving specific puzzles.

I gathered the requested specific data and finished the exam entirely around 11:30 PM. That means I completed the process in approximately 2.5 hours, without even reaching the halfway mark of the 6-hour time limit.

## Conclusion: Is It Worth $100?

In summary, for a $49 campaign, it was definitely a worthwhile experience — especially when it also included a certification like CRTSv2 (Certified Red Team Specialist). But under normal circumstances, would I pay $100 solely for the CRTA? I'm not sure. In terms of price-to-performance ratio, I believe the real value lies in the training content and the educational lab environment rather than the exam itself.

Best of luck to everyone preparing for the exam!
